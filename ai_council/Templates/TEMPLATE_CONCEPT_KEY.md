# Concept Key
## Template Reference Document — The Scholar

---

## Usage

This template defines the format for concept keys produced by the Scholar at session close.

A concept key is a derived document. It is produced from the verbatim transcript and never replaces it. The concept key distills the session into named, numbered concepts for future injection — allowing new sessions to navigate prior decisions without loading full transcripts into context.

The concept key captures reasoning, not just conclusions. A concept that records only what was decided without recording why it was decided will fail to prevent future sessions from relitigating settled ground.

The Scholar does not editorialize, interpret, or add content not present in the transcript. Every concept must be anchored to a specific transcript entry.

---

## Naming Convention

```
concept_key_[SESSION_ID].md
```

---

## Header Block

```
# Concept Key
**Paired Transcript:** session_transcript_[SESSION_ID].md
**Session Scope:** [One sentence describing what this session addressed]
**Usage:** Each concept is anchored to a point in the transcript. Use concept names to locate relevant prior decisions. Use transcript anchors to retrieve full context when needed.
```

---

## How to Use This Document

```
Read concepts in order on first use. In future sessions, scan concept names to locate where the current work sits relative to prior decisions. Use the transcript anchor to retrieve full context when a concept requires deeper verification. Concepts build on each other — earlier concepts provide the reasoning that makes later ones defensible.
```

---

## Concept Entry Format

Each concept follows this exact structure:

```
## Concept [NUMBER] — [SHORT DESCRIPTIVE NAME]

**Definition:** [One to three sentences defining the concept precisely. What was established, decided, or identified.]

**Transcript Anchor:** [Entry position and round reference — enough to locate the exact exchange in the paired transcript.]

**Connects To:** [Concept NUMBER (Name), Concept NUMBER (Name)]

**Why It Matters:** [One to three sentences explaining the reasoning behind the concept and what would break if it were ignored or overturned. This is the load-bearing section — do not summarize it.]
```

---

## Open Threads Section

Concepts that were raised but not resolved belong here, not in the numbered concept list.

```
## Open Threads

**[NUMBER]. [Thread name]**
[One to two sentences describing what was raised, why it matters, and what would need to happen to resolve it.]
```

---

## Conceptual Thread Summary

One paragraph at the end of every concept key. Written for fast re-entry — captures the full arc of the session in a form that can be read in under a minute and restore working context.

```
## Conceptual Thread Summary

[Single paragraph. Full arc of the session. What the session started with, what it arrived at, what the key turning points were, and what remains open. No bullet points. No headers. Prose only.]
```

---

## What Does Not Belong in a Concept Key

- Content not present in the transcript
- Summaries that replace transcript content rather than pointing to it
- Concepts without transcript anchors
- Editorial additions or Scholar interpretation beyond what the session produced
- Personal or sensitive information of any kind

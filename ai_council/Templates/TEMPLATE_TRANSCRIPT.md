# Session Transcript
## Template Reference Document — The Scholar

---

## Usage

This template defines the format for verbatim session transcripts produced by the Scholar at session close.

A transcript is a copy operation, not a writing operation. The Scholar reproduces the Paper in its entirety — every voice entry, every tag, every `{SYSTEM}` wrapper entry, in the exact order it appeared. Nothing is omitted, condensed, paraphrased, or summarized.

The transcript is the authoritative record. The Concept Key is derived from it. If a conflict exists between the two, the transcript governs.

---

## Naming Convention

```
session_transcript_[SESSION_ID].md
```

---

## Format

```
[VOICE/SYSTEM SOURCE] — [ROUND] — [ENTRY POSITION]

[Exact content of entry reproduced verbatim, including all tags]

---
```

Every entry follows this format. No exceptions. No editorial additions between entries.

---

## Example Entry Structure

```
{SYSTEM} — PRE-DELIBERATION — 001

[Steward opening entry reproduced exactly as written to the Paper]

---

[VOICE NAME] — ROUND 1 — 002

[Voice contribution reproduced exactly as written to the Paper, including any tags produced]

---

{SYSTEM} — ROUND 1 CLOSE — 003

[Wrapper system entry reproduced exactly]

---
```

---

## What Counts as an Entry

- All voice contributions regardless of length
- All `{SYSTEM}` wrapper entries including seat order declarations, round open and close markers, and `{Halt}` reviewed markers
- All tags produced by any voice — `{Warn}`, `{Vio1}`, `{Vio2}`, `{Halt}` — reproduced inline exactly where they appeared
- Checkpoint declarations from the Steward
- Halt Events and Halt Logs from the Protector

---

## What Does Not Belong in a Transcript

- Scholar commentary or annotation
- Summaries of any kind
- Editorial section breaks not present in the Paper
- Any content not present in the original Paper
